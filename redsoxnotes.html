BOSTON RED SOX (52-49, 3rd AL East, 7.0 GB) — OFF DAY

Boston is off today after a doubleheader split with Baltimore delivered both a tied franchise record and a snapped one: the Red Sox won the opener 6-3 to match the 1946 club's mark of 15 straight wins, then lost the nightcap 5-1 as Baltimore ended the streak at 15 — the longest run in the majors since St. Louis won 15 in a row in September 2021.

Boston's starting lineup for Friday had not been posted as of this writing.
Looking further out: after Toronto, Boston opens a 7-game West Coast swing at the Athletics (7/27–30, West Sacramento) and the Dodgers (7/31–8/2) before the Aug. 3 trade deadline.

State of the Sox
  • Boston is 52-49 (3rd, AL East, 7.0 GB) after Wednesday's doubleheader split with Baltimore, and still holds the American League's third and final Wild Card spot outright, 2.0 games clear of Seattle.
  • The Red Sox are 20-3 in their last 23 games and 23-7 over their last 30 — by far the best stretch of baseball the club has played all year, streak or no streak.
  • Tampa Bay leads the AL East at 59-42; Boston trails by 7.0 games with 61 to play. New York (57-45) sits second, 2.5 back of the Rays.
  • Today is a scheduled off day — the calm before a stretch run: 3 vs. Toronto, then 7 games on the road against Oakland (in West Sacramento) and the Dodgers before the Aug. 3 trade deadline.
15 Straight, Then Snapped
  • The Red Sox' bid for a 16th consecutive win ended Wednesday night as Kyle Bradish — making his first start after agreeing to a five-year, $90 million extension for 2027-31 — worked six innings of one-run ball for Baltimore in a 5-1 Orioles win.
  • Boston's 15-game streak ties the 1946 Red Sox (Ted Williams's pennant-winning club, which went on to lose a seven-game World Series to St. Louis) for the longest in the 126-year history of the franchise, and stands as the longest run in the majors since the 2021 Cardinals won 15 straight in September.
  • It was Boston's first loss since a 10-2 defeat to Washington on July 1 — 16 games and three-and-a-half weeks without a defeat before Wednesday's nightcap.
  • During the streak, Boston outscored opponents 5.9 to 2.1 runs per game; across the full 20-3 stretch since June 25, the split is a still-dominant 5.2 to 3.8.
Game 2: Rivera's Rough Night, and a Quick Trip to Worcester
  • Eduardo Rivera (0-1), pressed into the start with Ranger Suárez still not cleared, retired only two batters, allowing four runs on three hits and two walks — the first run scoring on his own wild pitch — before departing after 41 pitches.
  • Brayan Bello steadied things with 5.1 innings of relief (4 H, 1 BB, 5 K), and Jovani Morán, Ryan Watson and Alec Gamboa finished it off; Masataka Yoshida's fourth-inning RBI single was Boston's only run.
  • The Red Sox optioned Rivera to Triple-A Worcester the following morning. Alec Gamboa, who had been up as the 27th man for the doubleheader, worked a clean inning in his second appearance in three days.
  • Suárez was held out of the start due to lingering illness and soreness rather than a setback with the groin issue that sent him to the IL; he's targeted to return sometime in the Toronto series (7/24–26).
The Two-Run Tourniquet
  • Hold an opponent to two runs or fewer and the 2026 Red Sox are essentially unbeatable: still 34-1 in those games, with the lone blemish a May 12 loss to Philadelphia. Wednesday's nightcap (5 runs allowed) doesn't qualify.
The 5-Run Recipe
  • Boston is 38-9 when it scores five or more runs and 14-40 when it doesn't. Wednesday's nightcap (1 run) fell on the wrong side of that ledger for the first time since July 1.
Fenway, Still a Riddle
  • Wednesday's split (a home win, then a home loss) left the home ledger at 23-28 against a 29-21 road mark — the reverse split widened back out to seven games. Boston gets another crack at it Friday, with 3 of the next 3 at Fenway before a 7-game road trip.
Deadline Watch: Shortstop in the Crosshairs
  • ESPN's Jeff Passan reported this week that shortstop is "a position of importance" for Boston ahead of the Aug. 3 deadline, floating the Angels' Zach Neto, Houston's Jeream Peña and Washington's CJ Abrams as fits; Passan said he expects the Red Sox "to be aggressive."
  • Boston.com's Chad Finn is more skeptical a real difference-maker is available at a price the Sox would pay — Colorado's Hunter Goodman (31 HR) would reportedly cost a package starting with top prospect Franklin Arias, and a Francisco Lindor fit is complicated by the money already committed to an injured Trevor Story.
  • The middle infield has been held down in the meantime by the Tsung-Che Cheng (SS) – Anthony Seigler (2B) tandem, with Andruw Monasterio in the mix as well, while Marcelo Mayer (10-day IL) and Isiah Kiner-Falefa (10-day IL) work their way back.
  • The only trade of the window remains the July 14 acquisition of OF/DH Jahmai Jones from Detroit (PTBNL).
Odds & Ends
  • Blanked and blanking: Boston has thrown 10 shutouts and been shut out four times this year.
  • Coin-flip baseball: 3-3 in extra innings, 2 walk-off wins against 3 walk-off losses, and 10-12 in one-run games.
  • Cycle-logical: Andruw Monasterio's April 25 cycle at Baltimore remains the only one by a Red Sox hitter this season — fittingly, it came in the same 17-1 rout that kicked off Boston's turnaround against the Orioles.
  • Eleven days and counting: the Aug. 3 trade deadline is now less than two weeks away.
