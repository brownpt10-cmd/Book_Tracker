State of the Sox
  • Boston is 49-48 (3rd, AL East, 7.0 GB) after erasing a 6-3 deficit for a stunning 7-6 win over Tampa Bay on Saturday — the Sox' 12th straight victory, their longest winning streak since 2006 and just the 7th time in franchise history the club has won 12 in a row.
  • Boston has won 17 of its last 19 games, a stretch matched only by the 2004 and 2018 Red Sox this century, per longtime team communications figure JP Long.
  • Saturday's win also gave Boston sole possession of the American League's third and final Wild Card spot for the first time all season.
  • It's the first time all year Boston has been above .500; per Baseball-Reference, the Sox have not spent a single day in first place all season (their high mark remains "never led"), yet they've erased a 14-games-under-.500 hole (last, June 24) in well under a month.
  • Home/road splits remain lopsided even with Saturday's home win: 20-27 at Fenway vs. 29-21 on the road, a 9-game reverse split.
All-Star Recap
  • Four Red Sox represented Boston in the AL's All-Star win: LHP Aroldis Chapman, 1B Willson Contreras, OF Ceddanne Rafaela, and LHP Ranger Suárez (Contreras & Rafaela were injury replacements).
  • Chapman worked a scoreless 9th, striking out Home Run Derby champ Jordan Walker — the first All-Star Game shutout inning by a Red Sox reliever since 2013. He'd already passed Hoyt Wilhelm for the all-time relief-strikeout record on July 4 vs. the Angels.
  • Suárez did not pitch; he's on the 15-day IL (groin) and is targeting an activation as soon as Tuesday, 7/21.
Saturday's Wild Comeback
  • Boston trailed 6-3 entering the bottom of the 7th — the first time all season the Sox have won after trailing by three-plus runs at any point in a game (they were 0-34 in such spots before Saturday).
  • Newcomer Jahmai Jones, acquired from Detroit on 7/14, homered in his first start with the club to erase Boston's first deficit back in the 2nd inning.
  • Wilyer Abreu delivered the last two go-ahead swings: a solo shot in the 3rd and a two-run, go-ahead blast in the 7th off Garrett Cleavinger — his third and fourth homers in 24 hours, making him the first Red Sox player with back-to-back multi-homer games since Mookie Betts (2016).
  • Aroldis Chapman worked a shaky ninth — two walks on 12 balls out of 20 pitches — but closed it out for his 21st save.
  • Patrick Sandoval, in his second start back from a long injury layoff, was tagged for 5 runs on 9 hits over 5 innings; his rotation spot could get squeezed once Suárez and Early return.
Abreu Eyes History
  • With 15 home runs on the season, Abreu can make it three straight multi-homer games with a big Sunday — a feat accomplished only four times in MLB history, most recently by Jeff DaVanon in 2003.
The 5-Run Recipe
  • Boston is 35-9 when scoring 5-plus runs, but a grim 14-39 when held to four or fewer. Saturday's 7-run outburst fit the pattern perfectly.
Road Sweet Road
  • Even after Saturday's home win, the Sox are a lopsided 20-27 at Fenway against 29-21 on the road. For a team that sells out the Fens, home cooking has been the problem, not the cure.
Cuban Missile, Still Firing
  • At 38, Chapman became baseball's all-time reliever strikeout leader (passing Hoyt Wilhelm) on July 4, threw the All-Star Game's first shutout inning by a Sox reliever since 2013, and has converted his last several save chances — 21 on the season, 2.05 ERA, 38 K in 30.2 IP. The oldest flamethrower in the sport hasn't lost a tick.
Odds & Ends
  • Cycle-logical: Andruw Monasterio hit for the cycle on 4/25 at Baltimore, the centerpiece of a 17-1 rout — still Boston's biggest win of the year.
  • Coin-flip October rehearsal: the Sox are 3-3 in extra innings, with two walk-off wins against three walk-off losses.
  • Ten of Boston's wins have been shutouts; they've been blanked just four times.
  • Comeback trail: Boston has 19 comeback wins this season; Saturday's 3-run comeback tied the season high.
  • Marriage ball: rookie lefty Payton Tolle got married during the All-Star break, then still made time to catch the Home Run Derby and All-Star Game before jumping right back into the rotation.
Rotation Reinforcements Coming
  • Ranger Suárez (groin) is throwing again and could be activated as soon as Tuesday, 7/21; Connelly Early (elbow) has progressed to throwing from 90 feet and is on the right track, though no firm date is set.
  • Trevor Story is hitting and ramping up fielding activity; Isiah Kiner-Falefa and Marcelo Mayer are both slated for X-rays early next week to track their progress. Garrett Crochet and Roman Anthony remain slow going.
Trade Deadline Watch
  • ESPN's Jeff Passan reports Boston's approach remains "fluid" ahead of the Aug. 3, 6 p.m. ET deadline — the front office hasn't ruled out buying, though Chapman, Sonny Gray, Contreras and Whitlock could still be moved if the club cools off.
