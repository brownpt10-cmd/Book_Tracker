BOSTON RED SOX (50-48, 3rd AL East) vs. BALTIMORE ORIOLES (49-51)

Starting lineup not yet announced at publication time; Baseball-Reference's lineup page carries nothing past Sunday's Game 98.
Next: Orioles at Fenway through Wed 7/22, then Blue Jays at Fenway (7/24–26)
State of the Sox
  • Boston is 50-48 (3rd, AL East, 6.0 GB) after a 6-1 win over Tampa Bay on Sunday completed a four-game sweep of the division leaders and ran the winning streak to 13 games.
  • At two games over .500, the Sox are as far above the break-even line as they have been at any point in 2026 — Baseball-Reference lists "most games over .500: 2, last on Sunday, Jul 19" as a season high.
  • Boston has won 18 of 20 and 21 of 30, and now holds the American League's third and final Wild Card spot outright, a full game clear of Seattle.
  • The climb is the story: the Sox sat 15.5 games back on June 24, the low-water mark of the season. Twenty-five days later they are 6.0 back — 9.5 games of ground recovered.
  • Per Baseball-Reference the club has still never led the AL East this season; the "biggest lead" field reads "never led."
Sunday: Contreras and Gray Finish the Sweep
  • Sonny Gray allowed one run on five hits over six innings; Willson Contreras turned on the first pitch he saw in the fifth for a 414-foot two-run shot to the triangle in center — his 22nd homer and his second in three games.
  • The three-run third broke it open. Gray is now 12-1 with a 2.48 ERA, and Boston has swept four straight series: Angels, White Sox, Mets and Rays.
  • The Rays came to Fenway in first place and left having been outscored 28-10 across four games.
1948 and Counting
  • Thirteen straight is Boston's longest winning streak since July 1948 — the Ted Williams/Bobby Doerr/Dom DiMaggio/Johnny Pesky club — and ties that team for the second-longest streak in franchise history.
  • The record is 15, set in June 1946. Two more wins ties it; three breaks it. Baltimore stands in the way of the first.
The Two-Run Tourniquet
  • Hold the other team to two runs or fewer and this season is essentially decided: Boston is 34-1 in those games. The lone blemish came May 12, a 2-1 loss to Philadelphia with Zack Wheeler on the mound.
  • During the 13-game streak the staff has allowed 1.85 runs per game and held the opponent to three or fewer in 11 of 13.
The 5-Run Recipe
  • Boston is 36-9 when it scores five or more and 14-39 when it doesn't — a split that has defined the year. The current streak has simply lived on the right side of it: 5.85 runs per game scored, 1.85 allowed, a plus-52 margin over 13 games.
Fenway, Still a Riddle
  • Even after sweeping four at home, the Sox are 21-27 at Fenway and 29-21 on the road — an eight-game reverse split. The four-game sweep of Tampa Bay moved the home ledger by exactly one game from where it stood a week ago.
  • Nine of the next 12 are at Fenway. If the home number is going to correct itself, this is the window.
Baltimore, Back in Town
  • The Orioles were the victims of Boston's most lopsided win of the season — a 17-1 rout at Camden Yards on April 25, the night Andruw Monasterio hit for the cycle.
  • The season series is even at 3-3. Baltimore arrives on a seven-game run of its own, making this a 20-game combined winning streak walking into one ballpark.
  • The O's have a catching problem: Samuel Basallo left Sunday's game with right-shoulder discomfort, Adley Rutschman has been managing left-wrist trouble, and Sam Huff was summoned as a third catcher.
Odds & Ends
  • Blanked and blanking: Boston has thrown 10 shutouts and been shut out only four times.
  • Coin-flip baseball: 3-3 in extra innings, two walk-off wins against three walk-off losses, and a losing 9-12 mark in one-run games — only three of the 13 streak wins came by a single run.
  • Cycle-logical: Monasterio's April 25 cycle at Baltimore remains the only one by a Red Sox hitter this season.
  • Month of July: 13-1, outscoring opponents 78-34.
Rotation Reinforcements
  • Ranger Suárez (groin) is first eligible to come off the 15-day IL Tuesday, July 21; he threw long toss over the break and was scheduled for a bullpen to determine whether he makes that date.
  • Connelly Early (left elbow) sought a second opinion earlier in the month and has progressed to throwing from distance; no return date set.
  • Garrett Crochet still has not thrown a baseball since going on the IL on April 25 (shoulder inflammation, lat strain) and is doing rotator-cuff strengthening work — return projected no earlier than September.
  • Roman Anthony (partially torn tendon, right ring finger) is rehabbing in Fort Myers and is not yet swinging; August at the earliest. Trevor Story has resumed hitting and increased defensive work after May 22 sports-hernia surgery, with no rehab assignment scheduled.
Deadline Watch
  • Chief baseball officer Craig Breslow, on the record: "I don't think a 12-game streak should determine exactly what we do. But we've absolutely taken notice. We've put ourselves in a good position, and that's going to be very influential in how we approach the deadline."
  • If Boston adds, Breslow pointed at the lineup: "At times we've struggled to score runs. So, I think that's a place where we'll spend time." The health of Anthony, Crochet, Early, Suárez and Story are the stated swing factors.
