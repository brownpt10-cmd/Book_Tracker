State of the Sox
  • Boston is 46-48 (3rd, AL East, -10.0) but just 0.5 GB of the final AL Wild Card spot entering the break.
  • Riding a 9-game winning streak capped by a 9-0 road trip — sweeps of the Angels, White Sox, and Mets — Boston's first unbeaten trip of that length since 1977.
  • 14-2 in the last 16 games; 17-5 in the last 22, among the hottest stretches in MLB.
  • Road warriors: 29-21 away from Fenway vs. just 17-27 at home — a rare reverse split.
All-Star Recap
  • Four Red Sox represented Boston in Tuesday's AL 4-0 win: LHP Aroldis Chapman, 1B Willson Contreras, OF Ceddanne Rafaela, and LHP Ranger Suárez (Contreras & Rafaela were injury replacements for Guerrero Jr. and Judge).
  • Chapman struck out Jordan Walker and retired Matt Olson in the 9th, notching his 17th save and passing Hoyt Wilhelm for the all-time relief-strikeout record (1,364 K).
  • Suárez did not pitch — he landed on the 15-day IL (groin) just before the break.
Friday's Opener vs. Tampa Bay
  • Boston resumes with a doubleheader at Fenway (making up a 5/9 rainout) — starter breakdown, Page 2.
  • Homestand continues vs. Baltimore (7/20-22) and Toronto (7/24-26) before a West Coast trip to the Athletics and Dodgers ahead of the Aug. 3 deadline.
The 5-Run Recipe
  • Cook up five and dinner is served: Boston is 32-9 when scoring 5+ runs, but a grim 14-39 when held to four or fewer. The lineup, not the bullpen, has been the season's on/off switch.
Road Sweet Road
  • The Sox are 29-21 on the road but just 17-27 at Fenway — a 12-game reverse split. For a team that sells out the Fens, home cooking has been the problem, not the cure.
  • Their 9-0 road trip (first since 1977) outscored the Angels, White Sox, and Mets by a combined 48-14. Boston left home 11 games under .500 (37-48 on July 1) and returned a half-game from a playoff spot.
Cuban Missile, Still Firing
  • At 38 years young, Aroldis Chapman became baseball's all-time leader in relief strikeouts (1,364, passing Hoyt Wilhelm) in Tuesday's All-Star Game — and he leads the club with 17 saves. The oldest flamethrower in the sport hasn't lost a tick.
Odds & Ends
  • Birthday boy: DH Masataka Yoshida turns 33 today (born 7/15/1993) — spending his big day on the All-Star break.
  • Cycle-logical: Andruw Monasterio hit for the cycle on 4/25 at Baltimore, the centerpiece of a 17-1 rout — Boston's biggest win of the year.
  • Coin-flip October rehearsal: the Sox are 3-3 in extra innings, with two walk-off wins (both in the 10th) against three walk-off losses.
  • Nine of Boston's wins have been shutouts; they've been blanked just four times.
